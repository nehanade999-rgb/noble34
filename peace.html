Nobel Peace Prize 2025

Maria Corina Machado (Venezuela)
"For her tireless struggle to promote democratic rights in Venezuela and for ensuring a peaceful transition from dictatorship to democracy."

Nobel Peace Prize 2024

Nehon Hidakyo (Japan)
"For efforts to achieve a nuclear-weapons-free world and raising awareness that nuclear weapons must never be used again."

Nobel Peace Prize 2023

Narges Mohammadi (Iran)
"For her struggle for women's rights and human rights in Iran, advocating for freedom and justice over many years."

Nobel Peace Prize 2022

Alyes Bialiatski (Belarus), Memorial (Russia), Center for Civil Liberties (Ukraine)
"For representing civil society in their respective countries, documenting human rights violations and war crimes, and striving to protect citizens’ fundamental rights."

Nobel Peace Prize 2021

Maria Ressa (Philippines), Dmitry Muratov (Russia)
"For their outstanding work in protecting freedom of expression and defending the role of independent journalism under challenging conditions."

Nobel Peace Prize 2020

World Food Programme (WFP)
"For its remarkable efforts to fight global hunger and promote food security worldwide."
