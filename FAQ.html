Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Nobel Prize?
The Nobel Prize is an international award presented annually in several categories such as Physics, Chemistry, Medicine, Literature, Peace, and Economic Sciences to individuals or organizations that have made significant contributions to humanity.
Who decides the winners?
Different committees are responsible for selecting winners in each category. For example, the Nobel Assembly at Karolinska Institute selects Medicine laureates, while the Norwegian Nobel Committee selects Peace laureates.
Can organizations receive a Nobel Prize?
Yes, organizations like the World Food Programme have been awarded the Nobel Peace Prize for their efforts in promoting peace and humanitarian work.
When are the prizes announced?
The Nobel Prizes are announced annually, typically in October. Each category has its specific announcement date.
How can I learn more about laureates?
You can click on each prize category and then on individual laureates to read detailed biographies, prize motivation, and other resources.
